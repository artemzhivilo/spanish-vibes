# Lesson Text

Once you know whether a noun is masculine or feminine, you also need to make it singular or plural. Spanish uses predictable endings plus matching articles.

## Making nouns plural

- Nouns ending in a vowel add `-s`: `la casa` → `las casas`.
- Nouns ending in a consonant add `-es`: `el color` → `los colores`.
- Nouns ending in `-z` change to `-ces`: `la luz` → `las luces`.

## Indefinite article refresher

- Singular: `un` (masculine) / `una` (feminine).
- Plural: `unos` (masculine) / `unas` (feminine).
- Use the plural forms to say “some” or to introduce new items.

## Vocabulary

spanish | english | notes
---|---|---
un libro | a book | masculine singular
una mesa | a table | feminine singular
unos cuadernos | some notebooks | masculine plural
unas flores | some flowers | feminine plural
una luz | a light | becomes luces in plural
un color | a colour | ends in consonant

## Guided Practice

1. ___ (plural article) mesas están limpias. → `Las`
2. Necesito ___ (plural article) cuadernos nuevos. → `unos`
3. No hay ___ (plural article) flores en el jardín. → `unas`

### Convert to plural

1. `un libro` → ___ → `unos libros`
2. `la luz` → ___ → `las luces`
3. `el color` → ___ → `los colores`

### Translation drill

- “Some books are on the table.” → `Unos libros están sobre la mesa.`
- “We buy some flowers.” → `Compramos unas flores.`

```exercise
type: mcq
prompt: "Choose the correct plural: 'la luz' → ___"
options:
  - "las luces"
  - "las luzes"
  - "los luces"
answer: "las luces"
feedback:
  correct: "Exacto—z changes to c before -es."
  incorrect: "Z changes to c in the plural: la luz → las luces."
```
