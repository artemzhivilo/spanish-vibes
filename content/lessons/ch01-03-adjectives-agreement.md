# Lesson Text

Adjectives describe nouns. In Spanish, they must agree in both gender and number with the noun they modify.

## Regular patterns

- Adjectives ending in `-o` have four forms: `bonito`, `bonita`, `bonitos`, `bonitas`.
- Adjectives ending in `-e` or a consonant usually have two forms: `interesante` / `interesantes`, `ideal` / `ideales`.
- When an adjective describes a mixed group, use the masculine plural: `amigos inteligentes`.

## Placement

- Most adjectives follow the noun: `una casa grande`.
- Some common adjectives can come before the noun for emphasis: `un buen libro`.

## Vocabulary

spanish | english | notes
---|---|---
bonito | pretty (masc. sg.) | base form
grandes | big (plural) | same for masc/fem
interesante | interesting | ends in -e
trabajadora | hardworking (fem. sg.) | feminine form
rojos | red (masc. pl.) | colour adjective
pequeña | small (fem. sg.) | feminine form

## Guided Practice

1. `una casa ___ (bonito)` → `una casa bonita`
2. `los cuadernos ___ (rojo)` → `los cuadernos rojos`
3. `unas profesoras ___ (trabajador)` → `unas profesoras trabajadoras`

### Make it agree

1. `un libro interesante` → plural form? → `unos libros interesantes`
2. `un chico pequeño` → feminine singular? → `una chica pequeña`
3. `los amigos ideal` → correct form? → `los amigos ideales`

### Translation drill

- “The tall students are friendly.” → `Los estudiantes altos son amables.`
- “A small house is cosy.” → `Una casa pequeña es acogedora.`
