# Lesson Text

`Ser` and `estar` both translate as “to be,” but choosing the correct verb changes the meaning of your sentence. This lesson expands your descriptions from Chapter 1 by showing how the same adjective can shift when you switch verbs.

## The DOCTOR vs. PLACE lens

A helpful way to decide is to notice what you are describing:

- **Ser (DOCTOR)**: Description, Occupation, Characteristics, Time/date, Origin, Relationships.
- **Estar (PLACE)**: Position, Location, Action in progress, Condition, Emotion.

### Same adjective, different meaning

| Sentence with `ser` | Meaning | Sentence with `estar` | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `La sopa es fría.` | The soup is served cold (permanent trait). | `La sopa está fría.` | The soup has cooled off (temporary state). |
| `Ana es lista.` | Ana is clever. | `Ana está lista.` | Ana is ready. |
| `Los chicos son aburridos.` | They are boring people. | `Los chicos están aburridos.` | They feel bored right now. |

### Origin vs. location

- `Somos de Colombia.` (origin)
- `Estamos en Medellín.` (current location)

### Talking about people

- `Mi padre es ingeniero.` (profession)
- `Mi padre está en la oficina.` (location)
- `Mi padre está enfermo.` (temporary condition)

## Combining with nouns and adjectives you know

Re-use vocabulary from previous lessons to build accurate sentences:

- `La casa es moderna, pero hoy está desordenada.`
- `El profesor es paciente y está contento con la clase.`
- `Los estudiantes son jóvenes y están en la biblioteca.`

## Vocabulary for describing states

spanish | english | Notes
---|---|---
desordenado / desordenada | messy | temporary condition → estar
listo / lista | ready | use estar for “ready”
contento / contenta | happy, pleased | temporary mood
aburrido / aburrida | bored / boring | watch verb choice
moderno / moderna | modern | usually ser
ocupado / ocupada | busy | temporary condition

## Guided Practice

### Decide between ser or estar

1. `Mi madre ___ doctora.` → *es*
2. `Hoy ___ lunes.` → *es*
3. `Los libros ___ en la mesa.` → *están*
4. `El café ___ caliente ahora.` → *está*
5. `Nosotros ___ amigos desde 2010.` → *somos*
6. `¿Tú ___ cansado?` → *estás*

### Explain your choice

- `La fiesta es en mi casa.` → Event location uses **ser**.
- `La clase está en el aula 3.` → Physical location uses **estar**.

### Translation drill

- “The museum is open today.” → `El museo está abierto hoy.`
- “My cousins are tall and athletic.” → `Mis primos son altos y atléticos.`
- “Is the director in the office?” → `¿Está el director en la oficina?`

## Storylet

> `Somos estudiantes de arte. Estamos en Madrid este semestre. La ciudad es hermosa y está llena de museos. Hoy estamos emocionados porque la exposición es en el Prado. Después, estamos cansados pero felices.`

## Exercises

```exercise
type: mcq
prompt: "Choose the correct completion: '¿Por qué ___ tú tan feliz hoy?'"
options:
  - "eres"
  - "estás"
  - "son"
answer: "estás"
feedback:
  correct: "Good! Emotions go with estar."
  incorrect: "Temporary emotions use estar."
```

```exercise
type: classification
prompt: "Sort each sentence by the correct verb ('ser' or 'estar')."
items:
  - "La biblioteca ___ grande."
  - "El libro ___ en la mesa."
  - "Mis hermanos ___ ingenieros."
  - "Nosotros ___ cansados."
  - "Hoy ___ martes."
solutions:
  ser:
    - "La biblioteca es grande."
    - "Mis hermanos son ingenieros."
    - "Hoy es martes."
  estar:
    - "El libro está en la mesa."
    - "Nosotros estamos cansados."
feedback:
  correct: "Nice sorting—notice how description/time use ser while location/condition use estar."
  incorrect: "Remember: ser for identity/time, estar for location/conditions."
```
