# Lesson Text

After learning to describe people with `ser` and `estar`, you can now point out what exists around you. Spanish uses the single form `hay` (pronounced like English "eye") to express **there is/there are** with both singular and plural nouns.

## Introducing `hay`

- `Hay` never changes; it works for one item or many.
- Pair `hay` with indefinite articles (`un`, `una`, `unos`, `unas`) or number words (`dos`, `tres`).
- Use `no hay` to say something **does not** exist.

| Spanish | English |
| --- | --- |
| `Hay un libro en la mesa.` | There is a book on the table. |
| `Hay unas sillas en el aula.` | There are some chairs in the classroom. |
| `No hay profesor hoy.` | There is no teacher today. |

## Asking if something exists

Flip the sentence into a question by adding `¿` `?` and keeping word order.

- `¿Hay tarea hoy?` — Is there homework today?
- `¿Hay estudiantes en la biblioteca?` — Are there students in the library?
- `¿No hay clase mañana?` — Isn't there class tomorrow?

Use the vocabulary from Chapters 1–2 to build natural sentences:

- `¿Hay estudiantes nuevos en la clase?`
- `Hay una profesora simpática en la biblioteca.`

## Quantities and question words

To ask **how many**, combine `¿Cuánto/a/os/as` with `hay`.

| Question | Meaning | Sample answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `¿Cuántos estudiantes hay en el aula?` | How many students are there in the classroom? | `Hay diez estudiantes.` |
| `¿Cuánta tarea hay hoy?` | How much homework is there today? | `Hay poca tarea.` |

Common quantity words:

spanish | english | Notes
---|---|---
poco / poca / pocos / pocas | little, few | agree with the noun
mucho / mucha / muchos / muchas | a lot, many | agree with the noun
alguno / alguna / algunos / algunas | some, any | often shortened to `algún` before masculine singular nouns
ninguno / ninguna | none | rarely used in plural; combine with `no hay`

## Negative forms

- `No hay ningún estudiante en la biblioteca.` — There isn't a single student in the library.
- `Hoy no hay clase.` — There is no class today.
- To emphasize absence, pair `no hay` with time phrases you know: `No hay reunión esta tarde.`

## Mini escena

> `¿Hay café en la cafetería?`
>
> `Sí, hay café y hay té. Pero no hay azúcar.`
>
> `Entonces, hay que traer mi taza.`

## Guided Practice

### Decide yes or no

Answer with `Sí, hay...` or `No, no hay...` using the hint.

1. `¿Hay profesores en la reunión?` *(hint: two female teachers)* → `Sí, hay dos profesoras en la reunión.`
2. `¿Hay tarea hoy?` *(hint: none)* → `No, no hay tarea hoy.`
3. `¿Hay estudiantes en la biblioteca?` *(hint: only Ana)* → `Sí, hay una estudiante en la biblioteca.`

### Make questions with `hay`

- `Hay una cafetería en la escuela.` → `¿Hay una cafetería en la escuela?`
- `Hay diez sillas en el aula.` → `¿Cuántas sillas hay en el aula?`
- `No hay reunión mañana.` → `¿No hay reunión mañana?`

### Translate

- “There is a meeting today.” → `Hay una reunión hoy.`
- “There are many books on the table.” → `Hay muchos libros en la mesa.`
- “Is there a library nearby?” → `¿Hay una biblioteca cerca?`

## Exercises

```exercise
type: mcq
prompt: "Choose the best completion: 'En el aula ___ veinte estudiantes.'"
options:
  - "hay"
  - "son"
  - "están"
answer: "hay"
feedback:
  correct: "Correct—hay expresses there are/there is."
  incorrect: "Use hay when talking about existence, not ser/estar."
```

```exercise
type: fill_in_blank
prompt: "\"No ___ ningún profesor en la reunión.\" Fill in the blank."
answer: "hay"
feedback:
  correct: "Nice! 'No hay' states that nothing exists."
  incorrect: "Remember, hay stays the same for singular and plural."
```
