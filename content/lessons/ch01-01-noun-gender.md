# Lesson Text

Spanish nouns have gender, and the article you choose needs to match it. Learn a few patterns, but always memorise tricky exceptions.

## Spotting masculine nouns

- Most nouns ending in `-o` are masculine: `el libro`, `el vaso`.
- Nouns ending in `-ma` of Greek origin are often masculine: `el problema`, `el programa`.
- Days of the week and languages are masculine: `el lunes`, `el español`.

## Spotting feminine nouns

- Most nouns ending in `-a` are feminine: `la mesa`, `la ventana`.
- Nouns ending in `-ción`, `-sión`, `-dad`, or `-tud` are usually feminine: `la canción`, `la decisión`.
- Letters of the alphabet and many islands are feminine: `la hache`, `la isla Canaria`.

## Vocabulary

spanish | english | notes
---|---|---
el libro | the book | masculine, ends in -o
el problema | the problem | Greek-origin -ma
la mesa | the table | feminine, ends in -a
la canción | the song | ends in -ción
el profesor | the teacher (male) | person words show gender
la profesora | the teacher (female) | feminine counterpart

## Guided Practice

1. ___ (article) libro está sobre la mesa. → `El libro`
2. ___ (article) canción es nueva. → `La canción`
3. ___ (article) problema no es fácil. → `El problema`

### Article swap

1. `el vaso` → plural form? → `los vasos`
2. `la decisión` → plural form? → `las decisiones`

### Translation mini-task

- “The teacher is in the classroom.” → `El profesor está en el aula.`
- “The teacher (female) listens.” → `La profesora escucha.`
